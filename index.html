Let's Investigate the Average Attendance in Different Australian Stadiums by Year


The Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG) in Melbourne consistently boasts the highest attendance figures among all stadiums in Australia, year after year.

Despite the geographical proximity of other stadiums like Docklands Stadium and Traeger Park, the MCG consistently attracts nearly double the average attendance compared to these venues.

Did you know that in 2020, the Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG), typically bustling with over 100,000 fans, had no attendance due to the COVID-19 pandemic?

For the first time in its history, the MCG stood empty for AFL matches, including the iconic Anzac Day clash and the Grand Final, which was moved to the Gabba in Brisbane.

To fact check this, simply click on year 2020. You will notice no circular marks depicting MCG stadium.

Use the dropdown option above to toggle and view the average attendance of the different stadiums according to the year

Did you know that there are no major AFL stadiums in the northern, northeastern, and northwestern regions of Australia? This means that areas like the Northern Territory and parts of Queensland and Western Australia lack the large-scale stadiums found in other parts of the country.

This highlights the concentration of major sporting infrastructure in the southern and eastern regions of Australia, where cities like Melbourne, Sydney, and Perth host the majority of AFL games.

Let's Investigate the Relationship Between a Team's Attendance count and Weather Factors


How to read the chart?

Each vertical axis represent a different variable. Each line represents a specific match played by that particular team.

Trace a line through the different vertical axis to identify the relationship between factors. E.g Line starts high on Attendance axis and drops low on Maximum Temperature axis. Indicates high attendance during colder weather.

If many lines follow similar direction, there is a relationship between the 2 factors. If the lines are in random pattern with no clear direction, it indicates a lack of relationship between the factors

Use the dropdown option below the chart to toggle and view the relationship between attendance and weather conditions of different teams


Did you know that Melbourne, Carlton, and Sydney are among the oldest AFL teams?

These teams often show higher attendance figures represented by lines starting higher up the y axis, regardless of weather conditions, reflecting their rich history and large, dedicated fan bases

Did you know that Carlton, Collingwood, and Essendon are tied for the most AFL premierships, each with 16 titles. This intense competition has fueled some of the most memorable matches in AFL history where the rivalry between these 3 teams continue every season

These teams are so evenly matched that, year after year, their point progressions and points obtained each round are remarkably close. This leads to incredibly intense and exciting seasons for their loyal fans
